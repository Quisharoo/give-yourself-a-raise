## Design Context

### Users
The product is for the account owner connecting their own bank account data through Enable Banking. They arrive to answer one practical question: where can I realistically recover spending power without turning this into full-budget maintenance. They need clarity, trust, and a quick path from raw bank data to a credible recommendation.

### Brand Personality
Analytical, credible, unsentimental.
The UI should feel like a sharp financial instrument rather than a playful consumer budget app. It should reduce anxiety by being precise and plainspoken, not by being overly friendly.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode, trust-first, with a measured editorial layer for the brief and a denser analytical layer for diagnosis and exploration. The visual system should combine the calm surface discipline of a modern financial product with the operational clarity of a high-signal analysis tool. Avoid neon fintech styling, generic dashboard cards, and gamified money visuals.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with belief before advice: show that the model is credible before asking the user to act on it.
2. Each route should express a different product thesis, not just a different arrangement of the same blocks.
3. Keep the core promise visible: find the few discretionary levers that could create a 10% raise in monthly spending power.
4. Preserve inspectability without collapsing into dashboard clutter or spreadsheet density.
5. Use restrained emphasis: strong hierarchy, clear numbers, and selective accents instead of decorative effects.
